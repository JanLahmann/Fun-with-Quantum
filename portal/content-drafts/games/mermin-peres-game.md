Two separated players fill in one row and one column of a 3×3 grid with +1s and −1s. The parity
rules are chosen so that no pre-agreed classical table can satisfy them all — a short proof shows
it's mathematically impossible to win more than 8 out of 9 rounds. With two shared pairs of
entangled qubits, the quantum strategy wins **always**.

The magic square is the cleanest demonstration of **contextuality**: measurement outcomes in
quantum mechanics cannot be explained by any pre-existing table of values. The notebook lets you
try classical strategies first, then hands you the quantum one.
